\# References



\## Core foundation of LPLSP Method



\### \[1] A Transient Thermal Model for Power Electronics Systems



N. Padmanabhan, "A Transient Thermal Model for Power Electronics Systems," SoutheastCon 2024, Atlanta, GA, USA, 2024, pp. 1294-1299, doi: 10.1109/SoutheastCon52093.2024.10500091. 



DOI:

https://doi.org/10.1109/SoutheastCon52093.2024.10500091



Summary:

Introduces the Lumped Parameter Linear Superposition (LPLSP) framework for transient thermal modeling of power electronics systems.



\---



\### \[2] A Scalable Approach for Transient Thermal Modeling of Automotive Power Electronics



Padmanabhan, N., "A Scalable Approach for Transient Thermal Modeling of Automotive Power Electronics," SAE Technical Paper Series, January 1, 2025, https://doi.org/10.4271/2025-01-5073.



DOI:

https://doi.org/10.4271/2025-01-5073



Summary:

Extends the methodology to large-scale automotive electronics applications including case study of non-linear flow of forced convection with variable flow velocity. It also includes a sensitivity study of all the physical parameters of the equation and their effects on the model.
